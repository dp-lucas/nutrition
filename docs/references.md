# Research References

Evidence-based citations supporting dietary recommendations in this project.

## Format

Each entry follows this structure:

```
### [#] Short Title
- **Authors:** ...
- **Source:** Journal / Organization, Year
- **Key finding:** One-line summary relevant to this patient
- **Used in:** Which recommendation this supports
```

- **Used in:** Breakfast equivalency analysis — patient-specific product substitutions

### [3] Green et al. — Carbohydrate Ingestion Augments Creatine Retention
- **Authors:** Green AL, Hultman E, Macdonald IA, Sewell DA, Greenhaff PL
- **Source:** American Journal of Physiology-Endocrinology and Metabolism, 1996; 271(5): E821–E826
- **Key finding:** Ingesting carbohydrates with creatine significantly increases insulin-mediated creatine retention in skeletal muscle compared to creatine alone
- **Used in:** Post-workout honey + creatine timing rationale (`example-days.html`)

### [4] Staples et al. — Post-Exercise Fat Does Not Impair MPS
- **Authors:** Staples AW, Burd NA, West DW, Currie KD, Atherton PJ, Moore DR, Rennie MJ, Macdonald MJ, Baker SK, Phillips SM
- **Source:** Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise, 2011; 43(7): 1154–1161
- **Key finding:** Co-ingesting fat with protein post-exercise does not impair myofibrillar protein synthesis. Insulin reduces muscle protein breakdown, improving net protein balance
- **Used in:** Justification for 10g peanut butter in post-workout shake (`example-days.html`)

### [5] Jäger et al. — ISSN Position Stand: Protein and Exercise
- **Authors:** Jäger R, Kerksick CM, Campbell BI, et al.
- **Source:** Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition, 2017; 14:20
- **Key finding:** 20–40g high-quality protein post-exercise is sufficient to maximally stimulate muscle protein synthesis. Larger athletes may benefit from the upper end of this range
- **Used in:** Post-workout shake dosing (30g whey = 25g protein) (`example-days.html`)

### [6] Gorissen et al. — Co-Ingestion of Fat with Protein
- **Authors:** Gorissen SH, Burd NA, Hamer HM, Gijsen AP, Groen BB, van Loon LJ
- **Source:** British Journal of Nutrition, 2014; 111(3): 515–523
- **Key finding:** Co-ingesting fat with casein protein does not impair the post-prandial muscle protein synthetic response
- **Used in:** Supporting evidence for post-workout fat tolerance (`example-days.html`)

### [7] USDA FoodData Central — Chicken Breast Raw (FDC #171077)
- **Source:** U.S. Department of Agriculture, FoodData Central, SR Legacy
- **Key finding:** Raw skinless boneless chicken breast: 21–22.5g protein per 100g, 2.6g fat per 100g, 114–120 kcal per 100g. Used to verify dinner protein calculations (200g raw ≈ 44g protein)
- **Used in:** Dinner macro audit and correction (`example-days.html`)
