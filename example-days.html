Protein Distribution & MPS Timing

  • 06:30 — 1g P (pre-workout fuel, not an MPS trigger)
  • 08:15 — 29g P → MPS pulse #1 (post-workout, elevated sensitivity)
  • 13:00 — 46g P → MPS pulse #2 (4.5h gap — MPS has reset, re-triggered)
  • 17:00 — 38g P → MPS pulse #3 (4h gap — sustained afternoon synthesis)
  • 20:30 — 49g P → MPS pulse #4 (3.5h gap — slow beef protein feeds overnight repair)
  • Research basis: MPS peaks ~90 min post-ingestion then returns to baseline within 3–5h. Pulsing protein every 3–5h across 4 feedings maximizes 24h MPS (Areta et al. 2013, AJCN).

Supplement Timing Rationale

  • Creatine → breakfast (post-workout): Post-exercise muscle cells are insulin-sensitive and have increased blood flow. Carbs (42g) spike insulin, enhancing creatine uptake into muscle tissue (Antonio & Ciccone 2013).
  • Omega-3, D3+K2 → dinner: All three are fat-soluble. Bioavailability increases 2–3x when consumed with dietary fat. Dinner has 15g fat (from beef + oil) vs breakfast's 2g. Moving them here means you actually absorb what you're paying for.
  • Magnesium glycinate → after dinner: Glycinate form crosses the blood-brain barrier and has calming/sleep-promoting properties. Taking it 1–2h before bed supports sleep onset and deep sleep quality, which is when growth hormone peaks.
